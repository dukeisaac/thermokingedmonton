What does a transport refrigeration technician do?

A transport refrigeration technician specialises in installing, maintaining, and repairing refrigeration systems in vehicles that transport perishable goods. Their work ensures that items like food and pharmaceuticals are kept at the proper temperature during transit.

Key responsibilities include installing refrigeration units on various vehicles, performing routine maintenance to ensure optimal operation, and diagnosing and repairing any faults that arise. This involves tasks such as checking refrigerant levels, replacing components like compressors and condensers, and troubleshooting electrical or mechanical issues.

Technicians must also be familiar with regulations and safety standards related to refrigeration and transport, ensuring compliance and safe practices. Their role is crucial in maintaining the quality and safety of perishable products during transportation, making them an essential part of the logistics chain.
